Background

In 2020, there is a serious coronavirus disease (COVID-19) outbreak around the world, in which more than 1.36M people have been infected and nearly 76K people die from this disease, based on the statistics from Johns Hopkins University (last accessed: 7th April, 2020). The World Health Organization (WHO) has deemed COVID-19 a pandemic. To provide the first-hand information for the general public, many web-based visualization systems have been developed for visualizing the distribution / statistics of COVID-19 cases. However, these systems either cannot provide high visualization quality or cannot support interactive visualization operations (e.g., zoom in and panning operations). Kernel density visualization (KDV) is a solution to offer high visualization quality (for hot spot analysis) but its computational cost limits its usability. Here, we develop the first near real-time interactive visualization tool IKDV-SYS that is built on top of our prior study [1] on the efficient kernel density computation.

IKDV-SYS

IKDV-SYS can be used to visualize the distribution of COVID-19 cases (based on heatmap) in the world. Compared with the state-of-the-art geographical software, e.g., QGIS and ArcGIS, IKDV-SYS is the first web-based system, which can support:
(1) the near real-time kernel density visualization in single machine setting without using GPU and parallel computation
(2) different types of interactive visualization operations, e.g., zoom-in, panning etc, in near real-time
We have released our system (1st version) to the general public. However, this is only the prototype in which we still need to refine it as better as possible. As such, we disclaim any liability in connection with the use of this system. In addition, since we cannot obtain the precise dataset for representing the COVID-19 cases, due to the privacy issue. The visualization in the city/street level is not accurate. Our demo paper is under submission to VLDB2020 (demo track) [2]. Hope that this paper can be accepted in this conference.
